Brain Tumor

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells in the brain or skull that can be dangerous and cause a variety of symptoms:

Types:   There are more than 120 types of brain tumors, which can be benign (noncancerous), malignant (cancerous), or have a 50/50 chance of being cancerous.

Symptoms:   Symptoms include headaches, seizures, changes in mental function, changes in speech, changes in vision, loss of balance, and changes in the ability to feel. Headaches are often the first symptom and can worsen when lying down, bending over, or bearing down.

Causes:  Brain tumors can originate in the brain (primary brain tumors) or spread to the brain from other parts of the body (secondary or metastatic brain tumors).

Treatment:    Treatments include surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy.

Risk:    Brain tumors can be dangerous because they can put pressure on healthy parts of the brain, block the flow of fluid around the brain, or spread to other parts of the brain or spine. 

Rarity:   Brain tumors are rare, affecting less than 1% of the population during their lifetime.
